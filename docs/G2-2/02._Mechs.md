üóíÔ∏è<strong>Explanation</strong>

If a mech is solved corectly Kazeros will explode one side of the map which is determined by one of the chained people after they solve the mech with a target over them (they can point kazeros towards any direction). creating quicksand there which kills any players who step into it.</p>

## <ins>600x/150x</ins>

üóíÔ∏è<strong>Explanation</strong> 
<p align=justify>Kazeros have 2 specific mechs during this gate that can either take time of your gate or increase it. They happen at 600x and 150x and can never repeat, which means you will get both, but randomly decided which one first.</p>

<p align=justify>1. Elgic counter</p>

<p align=justify>Kazeros will teleport middle and begin a series of <code style="color : Darkorange">guard</code> and <code style="color : Blue">counters</code>. At the end he does a <code style="color : Fuchsia">co-op counter</code>. Failing it results in a raid wipe.</p>

<video width="800" height="500" controls="" muted="">
    <source src="https://github.com/Rubberoni/Kazeros-G2-Clips/raw/refs/heads/main/G2-2%20Clips/G2-2_Elgic.mp4">
</video>

<p align=justify>There are 2 variations in TFM (HM has only first one):</p>  
<p align=justify>2x <code style="color : Blue">Counter</code> -> 2x <code style="color : Darkorange">Guard</code> -> <code style="color : Blue">Counter</code> -> <code style="color : Darkorange">Guard</code> -> <code style="color : Blue">Counter</code> -> <code style="color : Fuchsia">co-op counter</code>.</p>
<p align=justify><code style="color : Darkorange">Guard</code> -> <code style="color : Blue">Counter</code> -> <code style="color : Darkorange">Guard</code> -> 3x <code style="color : Blue">Counter</code> -> <code style="color : Darkorange">Guard</code> -><code style="color : Fuchsia">co-op counter</code>.</p>
<p align=justify>Its important to know that only the person who counters will have its cooldown refreshed and if you fail a <code style="color : Darkorange">guard</code> you will get stunned for the remainder of the mech.</p>

‚úÖ<strong>Useful information</strong>
<p align=justify>Ideally you choose people with multiple <code style="color : Blue">counters</code> + long ones to do earlier ones so that they have their counters ready for last one.</p>
<p align=justify>The way he travels after every <code style="color : Darkorange">guard</code> and <code style="color : Blue">counter</code> is scripted as well. It means he will always rotates/dash the same way every single time throughout many runs. If you can learn how he moves you can preemptively position yourself in front of him just before <code style="color : Blue">counter</code> happens.</p>

<p align=justify>2. Elgic Up-Down</p>

<p align=justify>This pattern has 2 variations, but the main idea stays the same. Kazeros will do a <code style="color : Fuchsia">co-op counter</code> and make 3 people fly around with them either staying on the same level, going up or going down. They all move together and will end up on the same altitude. After they stop moving Kazeros will either glow dark or white. Then you either want to <code style="color : Blue">Counter</code> him (which makes him swap his colour to the other one) or not. There are 2 solutions to this one - i will explain both of them and you can choose whichever one you prefer.</p>

<p align=justify>2.1 Elgic Down version</p>

<p align=justify>Solution 1: People end up on lower altitude -> you need white Kazeros (he only attacks higher altitude) -> check which color is Kazeros | If white - good, hide behind | If dark - bad, <code style="color : Fuchsia">co-op counter</code> him so he swaps colour and then hide behind. In example below you want to <code style="color : Fuchsia">co-op counter</code> him if he is dark, because people are close to the ground.</p>
<p align=justify>Solution 2: Instead of looking at players you look at the Kazeros. He will dash 3 times while also changing his color (his 'afterimage') between them while people fly around. In example below he was 'Dark -> Dark -> Dark'. His last colour change (so only the 3rd dash) will determine which one you <code style="color : Fuchsia">co-op counter</code> - you want to <code style="color : Fuchsia">co-op counter</code> the same colour as his last dash. </p>

</video>

<p align=justify>2.2 Elgic Up version</p>

<p align=justify>Same principle.</p>
<p align=justify>Solution 1: People are in the sky which means you want <code style="color : Fuchsia">co-op counter</code> him if he is white.</p>
<p align=justify>Solution 2: His last dash was white (start of the video) which means you want to <code style="color : Fuchsia">co-op counter</code> him if he is white.</p>

‚úÖ<strong>Useful information</strong>
<p align=justify>For some more examples check Memorizer's video. He explains this mech with Solution 2 (and i think most if not all players will find it easier after getting used to the colours).</p>
<p align=justify>People who will be sent flying are the same ones who <code style="color : Fuchsia">co-op counter</code>. That means you can control who do you send - you also have a lot of time to <code style="color : Fuchsia">co-op counter</code> (~2 seconds), but dont greed it - its better to do the mech than cause a raid wipe.</p>

## <ins>500x</ins>

üóíÔ∏è<strong>Explanation</strong> 
<p align=justify>Just a simple DPS check. Deal enough damage during 1 minute for his additional bar to dissapear. You get additional timer if you manage to do it. If you fail it you lose time - which just means a restart.</p>

<video width="800" height="500" controls="" muted="">
    <source src="https://github.com/Rubberoni/Kazeros-G2-Clips/raw/refs/heads/main/G2-2%20Clips/G2-2_500x_DPS.mp4">
</video>

‚úÖ<strong>Useful information</strong>
<p align=justify>You want to use offensive armen (first one) on this and recast him. He will take care of >50% of the bar. First hit of armen is a close range attack followed-up by a beam that goes throught the whole arena - wait a bit to see his next pattern and try to use it instantly on a decent one without running out of time. His recast are projectiles which autoaim so you can use it right away as long as the pattern is not making him untargetable.</p>

## <ins>300x</ins>

üóíÔ∏è<strong>Explanation</strong> 
<p align=justify>Hardest mech in this gate. Kazeros will surround himself with a shield and activate a stagger check. You have to destroy his shield first to be able to deal any stagger at all. During this mech he will spawn lots of diferent explosions that you have to avoid - each time you get hit they reduce your stagger and damage by 5% up to 50%. There is also gonna be a <code style="color : Darkorange">guard</code> during all of this the moment orbs on the edge collide. </p>

<video width="800" height="500" controls="" muted="">
    <source src="https://github.com/Rubberoni/Kazeros-G2-Clips/raw/refs/heads/main/G2-2%20Clips/G2-2_300x_1.mp4">
</video>

<p align=justify>After getting rid of his shield and staggering him, he will activate one more stagger. After completing it he will shield himself again which you have to destroy. During this the same orbs will reactivate in the same direction to <code style="color : Darkorange">guard</code>.</p>

<video width="800" height="500" controls="" muted="">
    <source src="https://github.com/Rubberoni/Kazeros-G2-Clips/raw/refs/heads/main/G2-2%20Clips/G2-2_300x_2.mp4">
</video>

‚úÖ<strong>Useful information</strong>
<p align=justify>Supports should use Hyper awakening at the start to prevent people from dying if they fail guard and also to help with the shield.</p>
<p align=justify>You shouldnt tank anything in this phase if you can avoid it.</p>
<p align=justify>Waves that spawn right after the shield gets destroyed are fixed - they always spawn from 'Bottom -> left -> top -> right'. Best way to avoid them is to just hug boss on the top right and move clockwise every time wave in front of you disappears.</p>
<p align=justify>You should also learn which skills of yours you can cancel to <code style="color : Darkorange">guard</code>. All the explosions in this phase are para immune so just before <code style="color : Darkorange">guard</code> happens you should use something to not get staggered and then <code style="color : Darkorange">guard</code> towards the orbs.</p>
<p align=justify>You can also use 1-2 DPS hypers if you find your damage lacking.</p>
